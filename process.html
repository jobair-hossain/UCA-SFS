Selection and Onboarding

What happens after you apply

This page explains the anticipated review, selection, and onboarding process for ARISE applicants.

Notice: The ARISE CyberAI SFS program is pending funding through NSF Program Solicitation NSF 26-503. Content on this site reflects the proposed program as described in the submitted proposal. Final review and selection procedures will be confirmed if the program is funded.

The ARISE selection process is intended to identify students who are academically prepared, motivated to grow in cybersecurity and CyberAI, and interested in public-service career pathways.

1

Review eligibility and program fit

Before applying, students should review the eligibility requirements, academic pathways, and service expectations to make sure ARISE aligns with their goals and preparation.

2

Prepare and submit application materials

Students submit the required application materials, including academic and professional information that helps reviewers understand readiness, motivation, and fit for the program.

3

Application review

Applications are expected to be reviewed by program leadership and faculty using criteria such as academic preparation, technical readiness, communication, and interest in public-sector cybersecurity.

4

Interviews or follow-up review

Some applicants may be invited for an interview or additional review steps to better assess motivation, communication, professionalism, and potential for success in the program.

5

Selection and eligibility confirmation

Final selection is expected to include confirmation of program eligibility, academic standing, and alignment with the scholarship’s service-oriented mission.

6

Onboarding and advising

Students who are selected will receive guidance on program expectations, mentoring, academic planning, and preparation for research, internships, and professional development.

What students should keep in mind

A strong application is important, but so is alignment with the mission of the program. ARISE is intended for students who are serious about technical growth, public-service pathways, and long-term engagement in cybersecurity and CyberAI.

Questions before you submit?

If you are unsure about eligibility, readiness, or the review process, contact the program before applying. Asking early questions can help you prepare a stronger application.

Ready to prepare your application?

Review the application guidance, check the FAQs, and make sure your materials are complete before submission.
