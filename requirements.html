Eligibility and Expectations

Requirements for ARISE applicants

Students should review both eligibility requirements and program expectations before applying.

Basic eligibility

  • Be a U.S. citizen and eligible for a qualifying Scholarship for Service pathway.
  • Be enrolled in, or prepared to enroll in, an eligible UCA computing program connected to ARISE.
  • Demonstrate strong academic standing and readiness for advanced cybersecurity learning.
  • Show genuine interest in cybersecurity and government service.

Eligible programs

  • BS in Computer Science
  • BS in Cybersecurity
  • BS in Data Science
  • BS in Software Engineering
  • MS in Computer Science
  • Accelerated BS–MS pathway students may also be strong candidates.

Academic preparation

Competitive candidates should have a solid record in computing coursework and be prepared for a demanding program involving cybersecurity, AI, data analytics, and practical experimentation. The proposal highlights academic performance, coursework, and technical preparation as key selection factors.

Professional readiness

Applicants should be willing to participate fully in mentoring, research, labs, competitions, internship preparation, and career development. Students should also be open to learning about the federal hiring and security clearance process.

Service expectation

Students who receive the scholarship are expected to complete one year of full-time employment in a qualifying government cybersecurity position for each year of scholarship support received.
