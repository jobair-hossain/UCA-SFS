About the Scholarship

About the ARISE CyberAI Scholarship for Service

ARISE prepares students for government cybersecurity careers through integrated CyberAI education, experiential learning, and workforce-aligned preparation.

Notice: The ARISE CyberAI SFS program is pending funding through NSF Program Solicitation NSF 26-503. Content on this site reflects the proposed program as described in the submitted proposal.

Program mission

The ARISE CyberAI Scholarship for Service program addresses the cybersecurity skills preparation gap by connecting UCA students with a structured pathway into public-sector cybersecurity service. The program emphasizes the integration of AI and cybersecurity education, practical learning, and close faculty mentoring.

Who the program supports

The project is designed to support 14 scholars across two cohorts drawn from UCA computing pathways in Computer Science, Cybersecurity, Data Science, Software Engineering, and the MS in Computer Science.

Core goals of ARISE

Prepare CyberAI professionals

Develop students for government cybersecurity service through integrated education, research experiences, and applied cyber defense training.

Strengthen the workforce pipeline

Expand clear pathways from undergraduate and graduate computing programs into government cybersecurity roles.

Advance CyberAI capacity at UCA

Enhance curriculum, laboratory environments, and faculty-mentored research opportunities that build long-term institutional capacity.

What makes the program distinctive

FeatureWhat it means for students
CyberAI focusStudents study how AI and machine learning support threat detection, security monitoring, vulnerability analysis, and cyber defense analytics.
Experiential learningScholars participate in research, labs, competitions, internships, and applied projects.
Workforce alignmentThe program is oriented toward the SFS job fair, government internships, and placement into qualifying cybersecurity positions.
Cohort modelStudents benefit from close mentoring, peer learning, and a shared professional identity within a scholarship cohort.
