About the Scholarship

About the ARISE CyberAI Scholarship for Service

ARISE is designed to help students build strong preparation in cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, applied research, and public-service career pathways.

Notice: The ARISE CyberAI SFS program is pending funding through NSF Program Solicitation NSF 26-503. Content on this site reflects the proposed program as described in the submitted proposal.

Program mission

The ARISE CyberAI Scholarship for Service program is designed to connect UCA students with a structured pathway into government-focused cybersecurity careers. The program emphasizes integrated learning in cybersecurity and AI, practical skill development, faculty mentoring, applied experiences, and career preparation for public-sector service.

Who the program supports

ARISE is intended for students in UCA computing pathways who want to strengthen their preparation in cybersecurity, secure systems, data-driven analysis, and AI-enabled cyber defense. The program is especially relevant for students interested in combining technical growth, hands-on learning, and long-term public-service impact.

Core goals of ARISE

Prepare CyberAI professionals

Develop students for government cybersecurity service through integrated education, research experiences, and applied cyber defense training.

Strengthen the workforce pipeline

Expand pathways from undergraduate and graduate computing programs into qualifying cybersecurity careers in the public sector.

Advance CyberAI capacity at UCA

Strengthen curriculum, laboratory experiences, and faculty-mentored opportunities that support long-term student development and institutional growth.

What makes the program distinctive

Feature What it means for students
CyberAI focus Students study how AI and machine learning support threat detection, security monitoring, vulnerability analysis, and cyber defense analytics.
Research integration Students engage in faculty-mentored research and applied inquiry that connect cybersecurity, AI, secure systems, and emerging CyberAI challenges.
Experiential learning Students participate in labs, projects, competitions, internships, and other hands-on experiences that strengthen technical and professional readiness.
Workforce alignment The program is oriented toward internship preparation, the SFS job fair, and placement into qualifying government cybersecurity positions.
Cohort model Students benefit from faculty mentoring, peer learning, and a shared professional identity within a scholarship community.
