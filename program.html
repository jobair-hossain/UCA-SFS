Program Experience

What students can expect from ARISE

ARISE is designed to combine cybersecurity education, artificial intelligence, Quantum Machine Learning, research, hands-on experiences, and career preparation into one student-centered pathway for public-service impact.

Notice: The ARISE CyberAI SFS program is pending funding through NSF Program Solicitation NSF 26-503. Content on this site reflects the proposed program as described in the submitted proposal. Final program activities and expectations will be confirmed if the program is funded.

How the program is designed

ARISE is intended to help students move from strong computing foundations to more advanced preparation in cybersecurity, CyberAI, and emerging technologies. The program emphasizes layered learning, research engagement, practical experiences, and long-term career readiness.

Stage What students can expect
Foundational preparation Students build strong preparation through computing, networking, cybersecurity, secure systems, operating systems, data structures, cryptography, data analysis, and machine learning fundamentals.
CyberAI integration Students engage with CyberAI-focused learning through coursework, labs, and applied activities that connect artificial intelligence with real cybersecurity problems.
Research and applied learning Students may participate in faculty-mentored research, hands-on projects, cyber exercises, and experimentation that strengthen both technical and analytical skills.
Advanced topics Students may explore advanced areas such as cyber defense analytics, trustworthy AI, malware analysis, threat intelligence, secure software systems, and Quantum Machine Learning for cybersecurity.
Career preparation Students build readiness for internships, professional development, the SFS job fair, and transition into qualifying government cybersecurity careers after graduation.

Key parts of the student experience

CyberAI-enhanced learning

Students explore how AI and machine learning support cybersecurity tasks such as anomaly detection, malware analysis, vulnerability assessment, security monitoring, and cyber defense analytics.

Research and innovation

Students engage in faculty-mentored research that may include cybersecurity analytics, applied AI, secure systems, and emerging topics such as Quantum Machine Learning in cyber defense.

Professional development

ARISE is designed to support growth through mentoring, seminars, technical activities, career guidance, competition preparation, and broader professional readiness.

Hands-on learning

Students can expect practical experiences through labs, projects, research activities, applied cybersecurity exercises, and other opportunities that connect theory with real-world challenges.

Cohort and mentoring model

The program is designed to support students through faculty mentoring, peer learning, shared development activities, and a strong sense of scholarly and professional community.

What students may gain

Technical depth

Stronger preparation in cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, secure systems, data-driven analysis, and emerging areas such as Quantum Machine Learning.

Research experience

Opportunities to work with faculty on research-driven and project-based experiences that strengthen graduate-school readiness, technical confidence, and career development.

Career direction

A clearer pathway toward internships, public-service commitments, and government-focused cybersecurity careers.
