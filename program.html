Program Experience

What ARISE scholars will experience

The program combines coursework, CyberAI integration, research, competitions, internships, and workforce preparation into one structured pathway.

Training pathway

LayerRepresentative activities
FoundationsComputing, networking, security, data structures, operating systems, cryptography, and machine learning fundamentals.
CyberAI integrationEmbedded CyberAI modules in existing courses with security analytics and ML-based threat detection.
Experiential learningFaculty-mentored research, cyber range labs, SOC-style analytics exercises, and capstone or project work.
Advanced specializationTopics such as automated cyber defense, threat intelligence analytics, trustworthy AI systems, and quantum machine learning for cybersecurity.

Embedded CyberAI modules

Students apply AI techniques to cybersecurity tasks such as anomaly detection, malware classification, security log analysis, vulnerability analysis, and cyber threat intelligence workflows.

Research and labs

Scholars work with datasets, experimentation environments, and faculty mentors to build operational skills in data-driven cyber defense.

Competitions and certifications

The program includes competition preparation, technical seminars, professional development, and encouragement toward relevant cybersecurity certifications.

Internships and career preparation

ARISE scholars are expected to complete at least one qualified summer internship during the scholarship period and to prepare actively for the SFS job fair and government hiring pathways.

Scholar cohort model

Students learn as a cohort through peer accountability, mentoring, shared professional development, and engagement with student organizations and research activities.
