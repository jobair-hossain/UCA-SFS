CyberAI Scholarship for Service · UCA

ARISE prepares UCA students for government cybersecurity careers.

The ARISE program is a proposed UCA CyberAI Scholarship for Service pathway that integrates cybersecurity, artificial intelligence, laboratory experimentation, applied research, competitions, internships, and faculty mentoring to prepare scholars for cybersecurity roles in government service.

About the ARISE scholarship

ARISE stands for Artificial Intelligence Integrated Security Education. The program is designed to strengthen the cybersecurity workforce pipeline by preparing students for government cybersecurity service through integrated education, applied CyberAI training, research experiences, and workforce-aligned mentoring. The proposal positions ARISE as a pathway for students in computing programs at UCA who want to combine cybersecurity foundations with AI-enabled cyber defense skills. fileciteturn1file0

Eligible academic pathways

The proposal identifies the following degree pathways for scholar recruitment: BS in Computer Science, BS in Cybersecurity, BS in Data Science, BS in Software Engineering, and the MS in Computer Science. It also highlights an accelerated BS–MS pathway that can support advanced study and research. fileciteturn1file3

Requirements

Applicants will be reviewed through a competitive process. The proposal states that scholar selection will consider academic performance, technical preparation in computing and cybersecurity, demonstrated interest in cybersecurity, communication skills, commitment to government service, and compliance with SFS eligibility requirements including citizenship and academic standing. fileciteturn1file0

Scholarship support

The proposal states that support includes full tuition and fees, an annual stipend, and allowances for books, supplies, professional travel including the SFS Job Fair, and cybersecurity certification preparation. Support may extend for up to three years depending on the student’s academic entry point. fileciteturn1file0

Application and selection process

The proposal describes a competitive review process including application review, faculty evaluation, and interviews. Final selection must ensure compliance with SFS requirements and readiness for the program’s academic and workforce pathway. fileciteturn1file0

Step 1

Review eligibility and scholarship expectations

Students should confirm degree eligibility, academic readiness, public-service interest, and willingness to fulfill the SFS employment commitment.

Step 2

Submit the complete application package

Applicants will submit all required materials through the official process once the program site is formally activated.

Step 3

Faculty review and evaluation

The leadership team evaluates academic performance, technical preparation, communication skills, and commitment to cybersecurity and government service.

Step 4

Interview and final eligibility confirmation

Shortlisted applicants may be invited for interviews and final review of eligibility criteria, including citizenship and academic standing.

Step 5

Award, onboarding, and scholar counseling

Selected scholars complete program counseling and onboarding before scholarship support begins.

Step 6

Internship, placement, and service pathway

During the program, scholars are guided toward internships, job-fair participation, government placement, and fulfillment of service obligations.

Service obligation

ARISE follows the Scholarship for Service model: each scholar is expected to complete one year of full-time employment in a qualifying government cybersecurity position for each year of scholarship support received. The proposal also states that scholars are required to complete at least one qualified summer internship during the scholarship period. fileciteturn1file0 fileciteturn1file4
