CyberAI Scholarship for Service

Launch your path to a government cybersecurity career.

ARISE CyberAI SFS is designed for UCA students who want advanced preparation in cybersecurity and AI, meaningful hands-on experience, close faculty mentoring, and a pathway into public-service cybersecurity careers.

Why apply to ARISE?

ARISE is built for students who want more than coursework alone. The program combines academic preparation, practical cybersecurity training, faculty mentoring, career development, and a clear path toward public-sector cybersecurity service.

Scholarship Support

Reduce financial barriers while you focus on growth

The proposed support model includes tuition and fees, stipend support, books and supplies, professional travel, and certification-related preparation.

Hands-On Experience

Build practical CyberAI skills

Students engage in applied cybersecurity and AI learning through labs, research experiences, competitions, projects, and internship preparation.

Career Pathway

Prepare for cybersecurity service roles

The program is designed to help students prepare for internships, the SFS job fair, and qualifying government cybersecurity careers after graduation.

Who should explore this opportunity?

ARISE may be a strong fit for students who are motivated to develop technical depth in cybersecurity, AI, secure systems, and analytics while pursuing careers that support the public interest.

  • Students interested in cybersecurity and cyber defense
  • Students curious about how AI supports security operations
  • Students seeking research, mentoring, and professional growth
  • Students interested in public-sector impact and service

Eligible academic pathways

ARISE is designed around UCA computing pathways that support cybersecurity, analytics, software systems, and AI-enabled cyber defense.

  • BS in Computer Science
  • BS in Cybersecurity
  • BS in Data Science
  • BS in Software Engineering
  • MS in Computer Science

What students can expect

Faculty mentoring

Work closely with faculty through guided academic planning, research engagement, professional development, and career preparation.

Research and applied learning

Participate in projects, labs, cyber activities, and other hands-on experiences that strengthen both technical and professional readiness.

Career preparation

Build readiness for internships, certifications, the SFS job fair, and transition into qualifying service roles after graduation.
